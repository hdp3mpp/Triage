One-time setup · Takes about 10 minutes · Works on every device after

⚡ Complete this setup once and your tasks will sync instantly across your phone, laptop, and any other device — forever.
Step 1 of 4

Go to Firebase Console

On your phone or laptop, open console.firebase.google.com in your browser. Sign in with your Google account.

Step 2 of 4

Create a project

Tap "Add project" (or "Create a project"). Give it any name — e.g. my-triage. Click Continue, turn off Google Analytics if asked, then click "Create project". Wait for it to finish.

Step 3 of 4

Create a Realtime Database

Inside your project, look for "Build" in the left menu and tap "Realtime Database". Tap "Create Database". Choose any location. When it asks about security rules, choose "Start in test mode" and click Enable.

You'll now see a URL at the top of the page. It looks like:
https://my-triage-default-rtdb.firebaseio.com

Step 4 of 4

Paste your database URL below

Copy that URL from Firebase and paste it here, then tap Connect.

Why Pre-Loading Is Everything ▼ tap to read
The system only works if Sunday happens. 15–20 minutes every Sunday evening filling next week's windows. That one habit ties everything else together.
Your gold windows are finite and precious. Tuesday, Thursday, Friday mornings — roughly 9:30 to noon with an empty house. Everything important gets funnelled here.
Pre-loading is your highest-leverage habit change. When a window opens and you have to decide what to do, the squeaky wheel wins. Pre-load breaks that pattern. You execute instead of decide.
Cognitive work has no hard deadline — which is why it always loses. This system gives cognitive tasks a protected slot so they stop losing to physical urgency by default.
Two quick wins that cost nothing: Batch cooking Sundays + grocery pickup instead of in-person shopping could recover 5–7 hours a week. More than any other single change.
